Selection Activity

I failed my first ever exam in Year 12 (my second-to-last year of high school in New Zealand), which was algebra. The failed grade barred me from continuing into calculus in Year 13, my final year of high school. Without calculus, I wouldn’t have been able to pursue the degree I wanted without completing preparatory courses. Fortunately, I was granted a major exception by the head of maths and was allowed to continue. The failure came down to poor study habits, as I had previously coasted through school without putting in much effort.
I took a proactive approach by reaching out for help and discussing my situation with the head of maths. As part of the conditions for continuing into calculus despite the fail grade, I attended regular tutorials and consistently sought support when I needed it. These changes helped me build effective study habits and improve my understanding of the concepts covered in class.
This experience taught me persistence, discipline, and reaching out for help when I need it. By applying these lessons, I developed a much stronger work ethic which had me perform extremely well in calculus the following year, and I ultimately gained entry into the degree I wanted.
